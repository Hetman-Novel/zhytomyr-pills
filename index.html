How do erectile dysfunction medicines work?

All four medicines mentioned above belong to a class of medicines called PDE-5 inhibitors, which work by inhibiting the activity of the enzyme phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE-5). PDE5 is found inside smooth muscle cells in blood vessels and is responsible for regulating blood flow.

Erection depends on blood flow. By blocking the action of PDE-5, ED drugs such as sildenafil, tadalafil, vardenafil and avanafil dilate the blood vessels supplying the penis, improving blood flow and facilitating the achievement and maintenance of an erection.

Can erection pills be taken daily?

Some medications, such as tadalafil in low doses (2.5 mg and 5 mg tablets) as well as sildenafil (25 mg and 50 mg) can be taken daily for continuous erectile support. However, it is important to follow the instructions from your doctor and not exceed the recommended dose.
